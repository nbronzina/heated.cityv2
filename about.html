The honest page: what Cities That Care is, why it exists, and how it is made.

The project

Cities That Care is a design fiction project by Heated Studio. It imagines a near future — 2028 to 2030 — in which neighborhoods answer climate stress with coordinated care, and documents that future as if it were already here: an atlas compiled by a network of district coordinators.

Nothing on this site is a prediction. It is a rehearsal — a way to make one possible future concrete enough to be discussed, criticized, and maybe desired.

What is real, what is fiction

The cities and streets are real: you can walk Calle Defensa in San Telmo or the paths of Higashiyama today, and the climate pressures these neighborhoods face are real too. The projects, the people, the measurements and the coordinators' network are fictional — plausible extensions of practices that already exist in embryo: cooling centers, community cisterns, shared courtyards, neighbors who check on each other during heat waves.

How it is made

Each district is written from the perspective of its fictional coordinator, to give a sense of what everyday life might feel like in these places. The imagery — district renders and coordinator portraits — is generated with AI image tools and curated by the studio.

The site is hand-built as a static site, with maps by Leaflet on OpenStreetMap data, set in Fraunces and Inter. The protocols are extracted from the districts themselves: when a practice appears in more than one neighborhood, it earns a name.

Who

Cities That Care is made by Heated Studio and grows with the people who adopt districts. If the atlas makes you want to document your own neighborhood's possible future, the network is open — or simply reach out.
