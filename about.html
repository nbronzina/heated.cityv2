A speculative atlas of urban care infrastructure

Heated City is a speculative project by Heated Studio

Between 2028 and 2030, it documented fictional urban districts where care functions as infrastructure. The focus was on how communities might adapt to climate change through local responses like thermal regulation, water management, and shared spaces.

The project includes observations from imagined neighborhoods that deal with environmental stress through systems of support, coordination, and adaptation. Examples include cooling centers, adaptive housing, and biointegration sites.

Descriptions were written from the perspective of local coordinators to give a sense of what everyday life might look like in these places.

All content is fictional and intended for research and imagination

If you're interested in contributing or responding to the project, you're welcome to reach out.
