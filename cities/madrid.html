Plaza de Enfriamiento Serrano
Main Community Cooling Station

Most families don't consciously plan their pharmacy visits around the plaza's thermal cycles, but they've learned that certain times work better than others. Grandfather Luis consistently arrives at 4:30 PM because he discovered he walks more comfortably when the plaza is in its 24°C cooling phase. Three-year-old Carmen has internalized the fifteen-minute misting cycles and runs toward play zones when she hears atmospheric sprinklers starting. The plaza operates on standard municipal HVAC equipment—programmable thermostats and water circulation systems used in shopping centers, just reconfigured for outdoor community use. Local businesses adjusted schedules around thermal patterns without coordination. The bakery stays open until 6 PM during summer because families linger during optimal cooling periods. During monthly equipment maintenance when filters need replacement, families temporarily shift routines to the library or market, returning once repairs are completed.

The building uses standard commercial HVAC units with programmable zone controls, the same technology found in office buildings but configured to maintain three distinct temperatures simultaneously. Librarians adjust furniture layouts seasonally based on observed usage patterns—more study tables in cooler sections during exam periods, additional seating in warmer zones during winter when elderly patrons use the library as thermal refuge. When zonal controls malfunction several times yearly, staff temporarily redirect patrons to functional areas, usually resolving issues within 48 hours through standard municipal maintenance protocols.
